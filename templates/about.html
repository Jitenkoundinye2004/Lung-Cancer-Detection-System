Understanding Lung Cancer

Our AI model is trained to identify various types of lung cancer from CT scans, aiding in early and accurate diagnosis. Learn more about the types we classify below.

Types of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

Adenocarcinoma

Type NSCLC
Prevalence Most common type, especially in non-smokers.
Origin Glandular cells in outer lung parts.
Growth Tends to be slower than other types.

Large Cell Carcinoma

Type NSCLC
Prevalence Less common, but very aggressive.
Origin Can occur in any part of the lung.
Growth Grows and spreads quickly.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Type NSCLC
Prevalence Strongly linked to a history of smoking.
Origin Flat cells lining the central airways.
Growth Moderate, may form cavities in the lung.

Control Group

Normal (Healthy)

Meaning Represents healthy tissue with no signs of tumors.
Use Case Serves as the baseline control for model training.
Appearance Shows clear alveolar structures and no anomalies.
